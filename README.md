# Quantized interactions

This repository is a Rust-project to simulate aspects of quantum mechanics with networks of cellular automata and experiment with the rules that govern their behavior.

After watching the Veritasium item [There is something faster than light](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NIk_0AW5hFU), I thought: what if two entangled particles carry copies of a common state state is more complicated than the *outcome* of a measurement? I was thinking of a trick that is used on distributed systems named [consistent hashing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consistent_hashing).

I read on the Wikipedia page on the [electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron#Size) that the size of an electron could be something like something like 10<sup>-22</sup>m. That is much bigger than the Planck-length (10<sup>-35</sup>m). Could space be a collection of cellular automata each about the Planck length in size? Then an electron would be a pretty big thing that could carry a lot of information.

Then I thought that the cellular automata could manage potentials of the diverse electric, magnetic, strong and weak force fields. The way these automata are connected could model the geometry that defines space-time and gravity.

May be the wave/particle distinction can be modelled by encoding both waves and particles. Particles follow the wave potentials and the geometry of space-time and they interact when they are close. These interactions could send out new waves that interfere with other waves and modify future opportunities for interactions.

Can we come up with a model that has only local interactions, but still passes the [Bell test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell%27s_theorem)?

Can we reproduce the [double-slit experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double-slit_experiment)?

Can we model [Pair production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pair_production) and [Annihilation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annihilation)?

I even had a wild idea that a particle with mass could be an entity that disconnects automata from the regular network and launches them into space to find a quiet spot to reconnect. That would explain that massive objects attract each other (they eat the space between them), that massive objects bend light (they eat the space around them, thereby curving space) and the expansion of the universe (the disconnected space-particles have to reappear somewhere).

Can we model [Black hole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_hole)s?

Another wild idea is that a photon could travel with maximum velocity (light speed) like a smoke ring with a circumference of a fixed length.

Can we model a telescope that focusses light from the edge of the visible universe to get a picture, *i.e.*, can we distinguish between photons arriving with minute diffences in direction despite the graininess of the connections between cells?

So far I have been able to produce a circular wave in a hexagonal grid. To me that seems to indicate that a discrete nature a the Planck-level could look very smooth on a scale of meters or bigger and that artifacts on the lowest level need not even be discernable an a larger scale.

Let me know if you have more ideas on what to try or how to model it.
